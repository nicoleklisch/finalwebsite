Bruschetta Chicken

I am very much so a fan of traditional bruschetta.  Bread, tomatoes, and basil…how can you go wrong?  Since bread is strictly forbidden, I decided that I would use the next best thing…chicken.  The roasted tomatoes and garlic make the perfect sweet topping on the chicken and the balsamic reduction and fresh basil send it over the top.  If you choose to top with a balsamic reduction, and I hope you do because its amazing, I would definitely recommend having your air vent on while it simmers.  It tends to have quite a strong smell, but its is so worth it I promise!  This is the perfect sheet pan recipe for busy week nights that holds a punch of flavor.

Bruschetta Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 4 Boneless Skinless Chicken Breast
  • 12oz of Medley Tomatoes (Grape Tomatoes can be used as well)
  • ½ Cup of Balsamic Vinegar
  • 3 Cloves of Chopped Garlic
  • 1 Tablespoon of Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • ½ Teaspoon of Dried Oregano
  • 1 Teaspoon of Dried Basil
  • ¼ Teaspoon of Garlic Powder
  • ¼ Teaspoon of Onion Powder
  • 1 Bunch of Fresh Basil (Optional)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  1. 1. Preheat oven to 375˚F.
  2. 2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place the chicken breast about one inch apart from each other.
  3. 3. Drizzle ½ tablespoon on the chicken and season with salt, a couple cracks of fresh ground pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  4. 4. Slice tomatoes in half the long way and place in a bowl.
  5. 5. Drizzle tomatoes with the other ½ tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil.  Add the chopped garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and a couple cracks of fresh ground pepper.
  6. 6. Toss the tomatoes with the seasoning and oil and distribute the tomatoes in a single layer around the chicken.
  7. 7. Place in oven and set timer for 25-30 minutes, flipping the chicken halfway through.  Cook the chicken until it has an internal temperature of 165˚.
  8. 8. While chicken is in the oven, heat your ½ cup of balsamic vinegar in a saucepan on medium high heat.  Once the vinegar is brought to a boil, reduce the heat and let simmer for 5-7 minutes.  The reduction will be done when it coats the back of a spoon.
  9. 9. Remove the balsamic reduction from heat and set aside.
  10. 10. Remove the fresh basil leaves from the stem and cut into ribbons.
  11. 11. Take chicken and top with tomatoes, balsamic reduction, and fresh basil.
  12. 12. Enjoy…I obviously did!.
